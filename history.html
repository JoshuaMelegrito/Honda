About Honda Civic

In this page, you will gain another information about the selected civic’s that has been introduced.

Civic LX (2025)

The Honda Civic LX has served as the foundation of the Civic lineup since the model’s introduction in 1972. In its earliest form, the Civic LX represented simplicity and efficiency, powered by small-displacement engines that prioritized reliability and fuel economy during the fuel crises of the 1970s. As the Civic grew in popularity worldwide, the LX trim became a trusted choice for drivers seeking affordable transportation with Honda’s reputation for durability and practicality. Each new generation brought incremental improvements in comfort, safety, and efficiency, keeping the LX as one of the most popular trims in the compact car market.

In modern generations, including the current 11th-generation Civic, the LX has evolved far beyond its humble beginnings. Today’s Civic LX comes standard with advanced Honda Sensing® safety technology, modern infotainment systems, and refined interiors, while still offering affordability and efficiency. Though it is the entry-level trim, the LX embodies the Civic’s core values — reliability, fuel economy, and practicality — making it a staple choice for buyers who want long-term dependability at an accessible price. Over the decades, the Civic LX has remained the backbone of the Civic lineup, carrying forward the model’s reputation as one of the most reliable compact cars in the world.

Civic Sport (2025)

The Honda Civic Sport was introduced as a trim designed to inject a sportier personality into the Civic lineup without pushing into full performance territory. First appearing in the 10th-generation Civic (2016), the Sport trim built on the foundation of the LX by adding larger alloy wheels, blacked-out styling accents, a center-exit exhaust, and interior enhancements aimed at younger, style-conscious drivers. It kept the same efficient engine but emphasized design and driver engagement, offering a more spirited daily driving experience.

In the current 11th-generation Civic (2021–present), the Sport continues to play the role of a stylish and accessible option. With unique body styling, enhanced infotainment features, and a slightly firmer feel than the base trims, the Sport appeals to drivers who want more personality in their compact sedan or hatchback. While it doesn’t have the performance credentials of the Si or Type R, the Civic Sport has established itself as a bridge between practicality and sporty character, making it one of the most popular choices in the lineup.

Civic Type R (2025)

The Honda Civic Type R represents the pinnacle of Civic performance, first debuting in 1997 as a Japan-exclusive model based on the EK9 hatchback. That first-generation Type R featured a high-revving 1.6L VTEC engine producing 182 horsepower, stripped weight, and a track-ready suspension, cementing Honda’s reputation for creating precision performance cars. Later generations expanded to Europe with the EP3 (2001) and FN2 (2007), both equipped with naturally aspirated VTEC engines and lightweight engineering, appealing to purist drivers.

The 2015 FK2 marked the Type R’s return with a turbocharged 2.0L engine producing over 300 horsepower, while the 2017 FK8 brought global recognition by setting Nürburgring lap records for front-wheel-drive cars. The current FL5 generation, launched in 2022, refines the formula with 315 horsepower, a stiffer chassis, advanced aerodynamics, and precise handling. Each iteration of the Civic Type R has built on its predecessor, making it one of the most respected hot hatches in the world. Today, it stands as Honda’s ultimate expression of racing DNA in a road-legal Civic, delivering an unparalleled balance of performance and everyday usability.
